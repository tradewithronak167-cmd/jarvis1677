 (B)B.TECH WITH NO CSE:The B.Tech Non-CSE (e.g., Mechanical, Civil, Electrical) syllabus includes a mandatory first-year foundation in basic engineering and sciences. Core branch-specific subjects begin in the second year, replacing specialized computer science modules.First Year: Common to All BranchesThe first year focuses on fundamental sciences, mathematics, and basic engineering principles across all branches:Semester I:Engineering Mathematics - I (Calculus & Linear Algebra)Engineering PhysicsBasic Electrical EngineeringEngineering Graphics & DesignProgramming for Problem Solving (Basic C Programming)Semester II:Engineering Mathematics - II (Probability & Statistics)Engineering ChemistryBasic ElectronicsEngineering MechanicsWorkshop Practices
Second Year: Branch Core BeginsFrom Semester III onward, the syllabus diverges sharply into your specific discipline, covering primary concepts:Electrical Engineering (EE) / Electronics & Comm (ECE):Circuit Theory, Analog/Digital Electronics, Signals and Systems, Microprocessors, Control Systems.Mechanical Engineering (ME):Thermodynamics, Fluid Mechanics, Kinematics of Machines, Manufacturing Technology, Material Science.Civil Engineering (CE):Solid Mechanics, Surveying, Fluid Mechanics, Structural Analysis, Geotechnical Engineering.
Third & Fourth Years: Specialization & ElectivesThe latter half of your degree hards-skills your chosen field through advanced topics, internships, and capstone projects:Core Advanced Subjects: Deep dives into your chosen discipline (e.g., Heat Transfer for Mechanical, Power Systems for Electrical, Design of Concrete Structures for Civil).Open Electives: Non-CSE students often take these slots to learn foundational IT skills, such as Data Structures, Object-Oriented Programming (Java/Python), or Database Management (DBMS), to prepare for tech placements.Project Work: Major design or research project spanning the final two semesters.

(C)BBA-LLA:The BBA LLB (Bachelor of Business Administration and Bachelor of Laws) is a 5-year integrated program that combines foundational business management courses with core legal studies. It is structured across 10 semesters, transitioning from introductory principles to specialized electives and practical training
.Year 1 & 2: Core FoundationsThe first two years establish the fundamentals of both disciplines.Business Subjects: Principles of Management, Financial & Managerial Accounting, Managerial Economics, Marketing Management, and Organizational Behavior.Law Subjects: Legal Method, Legal English, Law of Contract (I & II), Law of Torts, and Constitutional Law
.Year 3 & 4: Specialized Law & Business StrategyThese years dive deeper into specific legal frameworks and advanced business concepts.Business Subjects: Financial Management, Human Resource Management, Business Environment, and Strategic Management.Law Subjects: Family Law, Criminal Law (IPC), Corporate Law, Property Law, Environmental Law, and Administrative Law
.Year 5: Practical Training & ElectivesThe final year focuses on procedural laws, practical advocacy skills, and specialized electives.Law Subjects: Civil Procedure Code (CPC), Law of Evidence, Drafting, Pleading, and Conveyancing, Taxation Laws, and Intellectual Property Rights.Practical Training: Moot Court exercises, trial preparations, and mandatory internships.

(D)BBA:The BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration) syllabus is a 3-year (6-semester) program focused on management, finance, marketing, and human resources. Core subjects include Principles of Management, Accounting, Economics, and Organizational Behavior, designed to build a foundational understanding of business operations.Common BBA Curriculum by ThemeManagement: Principles of Management, Human Resource Management, Strategic Management, Operations
Year 1: Foundational Business SkillsThe first year introduces fundamental concepts of business operations, basic economic theories, and essential mathematical tools.Principles of Management: Core concepts of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling business environments.Financial Accounting: Introduction to double-entry bookkeeping, ledger accounts, trial balances, and financial statements.Business Communication: Developing presentation skills, professional report writing, and corporate etiquette.Microeconomics: Analysis of individual market behaviors, consumer demand, and supply-demand pricing models.Business Mathematics: Basic algebraic functions, matrices, and calculus applied to common business problems.Macroeconomics: Broad economic forces including inflation, unemployment, GDP, and monetary policies.Organizational Behavior: Study of individual and group dynamics affecting workplace productivity and culture.
Year 2: Core Managerial ConceptsThe second year shifts focus to active managerial decision-making, marketing strategies, and processing business data.Marketing Management: Strategies for product development, pricing, distribution channels, and promotional campaigns.Cost & Management Accounting: Internal budgeting techniques, cost estimation, breakeven analysis, and variance tracking.Human Resource Management: Frameworks for employee recruitment, performance evaluations, labor laws, and retention.Business Statistics: Data sampling, probability distributions, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis for business.Financial Management: Capital budgeting, risk-return trade-offs, cost of capital, and working capital choices.Production & Operations Management: Logistics layout, supply chain management, inventory tracking, and quality control systems.Management Information Systems (MIS): Tracking how corporate networks, databases, and enterprise software drive decision-making.
Year 3: Strategy & Specialization ElectivesThe final year focuses on high-level corporate strategy, legal regulations, and elective courses matching specific career tracks.Strategic Management: Evaluating industry environments, competitive threats, and formulating long-term corporate goals.Business Law & Ethics: Understanding contract enforcement, corporate compliance, consumer protection laws, and moral corporate actions.Entrepreneurship Development: Designing viable business models, securing venture capital, and managing startup growth risks.Specialization Elective I & II: Advanced coursework chosen by the student in a specific field (e.g., Finance, Marketing, Analytics, or HR).Specialization Elective III & IV: Deep dive elective modules continuing the chosen specialization track.Research Project / Internship: Practical corporate application via a mandatory company internship and analytical thesis repor

(M)PHD MIN DURATION:Year 1: Coursework & FoundationDuring your first year, you complete mandatory taught modules and seminars to build advanced theoretical and methodological skills.Subjects: Research Methodology, Quantitative/Qualitative Research techniques, Data Analysis, and field-specific core subjects.Milestones: Passing qualifying or comprehensive examinations to become an official PhD Candidate.
Year 2: Specialization & ProposalThis year transitions from classroom learning to defining your exact research focus.Subjects: Advanced electives, specialized seminars, and directed literature reviews.Milestones: Writing, defending, and submitting your PhD Research Proposal or Prospectus.
Year 3: Primary Data Collection & AnalysisYour third year is typically free from coursework and dedicated completely to independent research.Subjects: No formal subjects; focus shifts to lab work, fieldwork, or archival study.Milestones: Collecting data, conducting experiments, and potentially publishing preliminary findings in academic journals.
Year 4 & Beyond: Dissertation & DefenseThe final phase focuses exclusively on compiling and defending your original contribution to the field.Subjects: No formal coursework. You write the chapters of your dissertation.Milestones: Drafting the thesis, finalizing revisions with your supervisor, and the final viva voce (oral defense).
